üö® If You Are Scammed - What to Do

‚ö†Ô∏è Immediate Actions (Do These First!)

If you've just been scammed, take these steps immediately to minimize damage and increase the chances of recovering your losses.

1 Freeze Your Accounts

Contact your bank immediately to freeze your credit/debit cards and bank accounts. This prevents further unauthorized transactions.

2 Report to Cybercrime Portal

Report the incident immediately through our Cybercrime Portal to get an official case number for your records.

3 Change All Passwords

Change passwords for email, banking, social media, and any other compromised accounts. Use strong, unique passwords for each.

4 Scan for Malware

Run a full antivirus scan on all devices. Remove any suspicious apps or software that might have been installed by the attacker.

5 Keep Evidence

Save screenshots, transaction IDs, suspicious messages, and any communication with the scammers. This helps with investigation.

6 Contact Law Enforcement

File a formal complaint with your local cybercrime police station. Provide all evidence and case details from the cybercrime portal.

7 Monitor Your Accounts

Regularly check your bank statements and credit reports for any unauthorized activity. Set up account alerts for unusual transactions.

8 Contact Credit Bureau

Place a fraud alert on your credit report to prevent criminals from opening accounts in your name. This is free and effective.
